Laws of Design
1. Hick's Law

Decision-making time increases with the number/complexity of options

Here, Apple makes use of Hick's law by greatly limiting what they present to the users visiting their site. They simply put two options for the user to choose from, buying the product and learning more about the product.
2. Law of Proximity

Proximity helps users understand and organize information faster and more efficiently

This example from IBM shows how they grouped elements together based on categories, to make it easy for the user to navigate the many different click-able links on the page.
3. Tesler's Law

There is always a certain amount of complexity that can't be reduced

The Maaemo restaurant website shows a minimal design where it has only the essentials. If they removed any more complexity, it would deminish the quality of the page.
