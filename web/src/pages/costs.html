Costs
Labor and Maintenance

We estimate labor and maintenance to cost us about $500/year for the current version as just upkeep. Moving forward with the project would require a few full-time developers and electrical engineers to improve the sensors and application.

Raw Material

Our current prototype costs about $35 to produce, which is a kit of sensors we aim to sell direct to consumers.

Revenue
Hardware

The initial hardware sales would be an optional kit of four sensors. This would be a one-time purchase the customer makes and requires the software to work. Customer surveys tell us we could sell these kits for around $50 retail. There would also be an option to purchase individual sensors that can be added to the app, for example to track multiple laundry baskets.

Software

The application would have a recurring monthly fee and would not require the hardware in order to work. The cheapest package will be offered at $30/year. Without sensors the users can still assign chores and use the point system, but will not have sensors that verify chore progress or completion
