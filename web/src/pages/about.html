(eye-vehn  ah-le-han-dro  roe-bless)

When I turned 2 years old, my mother immigrated to the United States with me and my two older brothers. With nothing but the clothes on our backs, we began our new lives. When I turned 13, I began helping my mom clean houses on the weekends, while going to high school during the week. When I turned 18, I got my first full-time job, became financially independent, and began earning my bachelor's degree for Computer Engineering from UCSD. It took me 23 years to become a software engineer, but it was worth it.

I genuinely enjoy software engineering. It's like solving a puzzle: you evaluate your givens, check for edge cases, and use logic to determine the validity of possible solutions. However, I also love creating beautiful user interfaces and experiences, which I fully believe is a form of art. Whether it comes from CSS or a pen, I always get a huge sense of satisfaction when people enjoy my creations.

Outside of my family and career, my biggest goal is to care for dogs. I got my 1st dog my 3rd year of college, adopted a 2nd dog a year later, and rescued a stray right after graduating. My ambition is to rescue as many dogs as possible throughout my life. You can see one of my dogs in this picture, or you can head over to my interactive resume to see my dogs as drawings!
