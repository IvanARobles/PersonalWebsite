Basic Information Architecture
Parents

Parents need to create chores and assign them to kids

Parents need to see live updates on the chores

Parents need to have control over the points the kids earn

Kids

Kids need to see their chores and be able to mark them done

Kids need to be able to see their points and try to redeem them

Design Principles
Principle of Objects

To make it easy to navigate, similar objects will keep consistent structures. We will use this by making Children users appear the same as each other, as well as different tasks, just with different labels and possibly different colors.

Principle of Disclosure

We essentially want to make organizing chores as easy as possible. Therefore we will limit the input that users have to provide as much as possible, and present it to them in layers to make only one decision at a time.

Principle of Multiple Classifications

Parents will be given two different views of the upcoming tasks, all with the push of a button. One option is presenting the information divided by children, each with their chores, to make it easy to check on specific children. The other view being a chronological one to display the upcoming chores sequentially, in case parents care more about things getting done on time, rather than by whom.

Structural Organizational Principles
Topological

The first organization involes topics, namely the children. This will make it easy to navigate the potentially many chores created and see who they are assigned to.

Chronological

The second organization involves time, namely upcoming days that have chores assigned in them. This makes it easy to ensure chores are being handled in a timely manner.
